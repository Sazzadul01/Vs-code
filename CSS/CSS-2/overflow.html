In a small town nestled between rolling hills, there lived an old clockmaker named Elias. His workshop sat on the corner of the cobblestone square, filled with the rhythmic ticking of countless clocks. Elias wasn’t just a craftsman—his clocks were said to hold a bit of magic, though no one could quite prove it.

One winter evening, as snow fell lightly outside, a young girl named Sophie stepped into his shop. She held a broken pocket watch, the gold casing scratched and the hands frozen at ten past three. “This belonged to my father,” she said softly. “Can you fix it?” Elias studied the watch carefully, his fingers tracing the worn edges. “This is no ordinary watch,” he murmured. “It has a heart.” Sophie blinked. “A heart?”
